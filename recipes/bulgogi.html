Bulgogi Recipes

Bulgogi is a popular Korean dish made from thinly sliced beef that is marinated and grilled. The marinade typically includes soy sauce, sugar, sesame oil, garlic, and onions, which give the beef a sweet and savory flavor. The meat is often ribeye or sirloin, marinated for several hours to let the flavors soak in.

This dish is commonly served with rice, lettuce wraps, or traditional Korean sides like kimchi. Bulgogi is known for its rich taste and is easy to prepare, making it a favorite in Korean cuisine.

Ingredients

  • 1 cup soy sauce
  • Â½ cup pear juice or white wine
  • 3 tablespoons white sugar
  • 2 tablespoons chopped garlic
  • 1 teaspoon sesame oil
  • 1 teaspoon sesame seeds
  • 1 tablespoon ground black pepper
  • 1 teaspoon monosodium glutamate
  • 1 (2 pound) beef rump roast, sliced into thin strips
  • 1 onion, cut into thin strips

Directions

  1. In a large bowl, mix together soy sauce, pear juice, sugar, garlic, sesame oil, sesame seeds, black pepper, and monosodium glutamate; add beef and onions. Stir to coat, cover, and refrigerate for 1 hour.
  2. Preheat a grill pan over high heat and brush with oil; add beef and onions. Cook, turning to brown evenly, for 3 to 6 minutes, or until done.
